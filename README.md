# AC System Buying Guide: What South Florida Homeowners Need to Know

> A practical, no-nonsense guide to selecting, sizing, and installing the right air conditioning system for South Florida's unique climate challenges.

## Why This Guide Exists

Buying a new AC system in South Florida isn't like buying one anywhere else in the country. Year-round cooling demand, extreme humidity, salt air corrosion, and some of the highest electricity rates in the Southeast mean that the wrong choice costs you thousands over a system's 15-20 year lifespan.

This guide distills what actually matters — no brand loyalty, no sales tactics, just the factors that determine whether you'll be comfortable and cost-efficient for the next two decades.

## The Sizing Problem

**This is where 60% of bad AC purchases happen.**

Most contractors size systems based on rules of thumb: "one ton per 400-600 square feet." In South Florida, this is dangerously imprecise. A proper **Manual J load calculation** considers:

| Factor | Why It Matters in South Florida |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Window orientation | West-facing windows add massive heat load in afternoon |
| Insulation R-value | Older Florida homes often have R-13 or less in walls |
| Ceiling height | Vaulted ceilings in many Florida homes increase volume significantly |
| Ductwork location | Attic ducts in 150°F+ space = major efficiency loss |
| Number of occupants | Each person adds ~400 BTU/hr of heat |
| Roof color/material | Dark tile roofs absorb significantly more heat than light-colored metal |

### Oversizing: The Silent Killer

An oversized AC system is **worse** than an undersized one in Florida. Here's why:

- **Short cycling**: System reaches temperature setpoint too quickly, shutting off before removing humidity
- **Humidity problems**: Florida's real enemy is humidity, not just heat. Short cycles = 65-75% indoor humidity
- **Increased wear**: Compressor startups are the hardest moment for the system — more starts = faster failure
- **Higher energy bills**: Startup surge uses 3-5x running current

### Getting It Right

Insist on a room-by-room Manual J calculation. Software like Wrightsoft or CoolCalc takes about 2 hours for a thorough assessment. Any contractor who won't do this isn't worth hiring.

## Efficiency Ratings Decoded: SEER2

As of January 2023, the industry shifted from SEER to **SEER2**, which uses the M1 blower testing protocol — more representative of real-world performance with typical static pressure in ductwork.

**South Florida minimums (DOE South Region):**
- Split systems: 15.0 SEER2
- Packaged systems: 14.3 SEER2

### What Higher SEER2 Actually Saves

Assumptions: 2,000 sq ft home, 3-ton system, 2,800 cooling hours/year, $0.14/kWh (FPL average)

| SEER2 Rating | Est. Annual Cost | Monthly Summer Peak | 15-Year Total |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| 15 (minimum) | $2,160 | $225 | $32,400 |
| 17 | $1,905 | $200 | $28,575 |
| 20 | $1,620 | $170 | $24,300 |
| 24 | $1,350 | $140 | $20,250 |

The jump from 15 to 20 SEER2 saves roughly $8,100 over 15 years. If the higher-efficiency unit costs $3,000-4,000 more upfront, the payback period is 5-7 years.

### Variable Speed vs. Single Stage

- **Single stage**: Full blast or off. Cheapest upfront but least efficient and worst at dehumidification.
- **Two stage**: High and low. Better humidity control, 10-15% more efficient.
- **Variable speed (inverter)**: Continuously adjusts output from 25-100%. Best efficiency, best humidity control, quietest operation. Found in 18+ SEER2 units.

In South Florida, variable speed is the strongest recommendation for homes over 1,500 sq ft. For the full engineering picture — sensible vs. latent load, runtime fractions, and how to read a quote critically — see the [Compressor Staging Deep Dive](docs/compressor-staging-deep-dive.md). When in doubt, a [professional AC installation](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-installation/) assessment with a proper Manual J calculation is the only reliable way to match staging to your home.

## Ductwork: The Hidden Variable

Your ductwork is as important as the AC unit itself. A $15,000 system connected to leaky, poorly designed ducts delivers $8,000 worth of performance.

### Common Florida Ductwork Problems

1. **Attic flex duct**: Most Florida homes use flexible ductwork in unconditioned attics. Even "properly" installed flex duct degrades over 15-20 years — connections loosen, insulation compresses, and condensation accumulates.

2. **Duct leakage**: The average Florida home leaks 25-40% of conditioned air through duct connections. At attic temperatures of 130-160°F, even small leaks waste enormous energy.

3. **Undersized returns**: Many Florida homes have inadequate return air pathways, creating negative pressure that pulls hot attic air through ceiling fixtures and gaps.

A [professional duct inspection and cleaning](https://ac-repair.today/services/duct-cleaning/) before installing new equipment can identify whether your existing ductwork will support the new system or needs modification.

### Duct Improvements Worth Considering

- **Duct sealing (Aeroseal or mastic)**: $1,500-3,000. Seals leaks from inside. Often qualifies for utility rebates.
- **Duct replacement**: $3,000-7,000. Necessary if existing ducts are crushed, water-damaged, or improperly sized.
- **Duct relocation to conditioned space**: $5,000-15,000. The gold standard — moves ducts inside the building envelope. Dramatically improves efficiency.

## Heat Pump vs. Traditional AC

Traditional split systems pair an outdoor condenser with an indoor evaporator coil and air handler. The air handler typically includes electric resistance heat strips for the few cold nights.

**Heat pumps** use the same refrigeration cycle in reverse — extracting heat from outdoor air and moving it inside. Modern heat pumps work efficiently down to outdoor temperatures of 25-30°F, which South Florida essentially never reaches.

### The South Florida Calculation

| Factor | Traditional AC + Heat Strips | Heat Pump |
|--------|------------------------------|-----------|
| Cooling efficiency | Identical | Identical |
| Heating efficiency | COP 1.0 (resistance) | COP 2.5-4.0 |
| Upfront cost | Baseline | +10-15% |
| Heating season cost | $150-300/winter | $40-90/winter |
| Federal tax credit eligible | No (AC only) | Yes (up to $2,000) |
| FPL rebate eligible | Limited | Yes |

For most South Florida homeowners, a heat pump makes financial sense within 3-5 years, especially with available tax credits and utility rebates.

## Indoor Air Quality Integration

South Florida's climate creates specific [indoor air quality challenges](https://ac-repair.today/services/indoor-air-quality/) that your AC system can address:

### Humidity Control

Target: 45-55% relative humidity indoors. Standard AC provides some dehumidification, but in Florida's shoulder seasons (spring and fall), temperatures may not require much cooling while humidity remains extremely high.

**Solutions:**
- Variable-speed systems that can run at low speed for extended dehumidification cycles
- Whole-home dehumidifiers ($1,500-2,500 installed) that work independently of the AC
- Smart thermostats with humidity-priority modes

### Mold Prevention

Florida's #1 indoor air quality threat. Mold spores are omnipresent outdoors and only need moisture and time to establish colonies indoors.

**Integrated solutions:**
- UV-C germicidal lamps in the air handler ($300-800) — kill mold and bacteria passing through
- MERV-13 filters (upgrade from standard MERV-8) — capture 85%+ of mold spores
- Proper system sizing that avoids short cycling (see Sizing section above)

### Filtration Upgrades

| Filter Type | MERV Rating | Captures | Cost/Year |
|------------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Standard fiberglass | 4-6 | Large dust only | $30-60 |
| Pleated | 8-11 | Pollen, dust mites | $60-120 |
| High-efficiency pleated | 13-16 | Bacteria, smoke, fine particles | $120-240 |
| HEPA (requires modification) | 17-20 | Viruses, ultra-fine particles | $200-400 |

**Important**: Don't install a higher MERV filter without confirming your system can handle the increased static pressure. A MERV-16 filter on a system designed for MERV-8 will restrict airflow and damage the compressor.

## Getting Quotes Right

### What Every Quote Should Include

- [ ] Manual J load calculation
- [ ] Equipment model numbers (not just brand and tonnage)
- [ ] SEER2 rating of specific models quoted
- [ ] Ductwork assessment and any modifications
- [ ] Permit costs (required in Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach)
- [ ] Old unit disposal
- [ ] Refrigerant line replacement (if existing lines are >15 years old)
- [ ] Thermostat (specify model)
- [ ] Warranty terms (parts, labor, compressor)
- [ ] Start date and completion timeline
- [ ] Payment terms

### Red Flags

- Sizing based on square footage alone
- "Today only" pricing pressure
- No permit mentioned (it's legally required)
- Won't provide equipment model numbers
- Warranty requires purchasing a maintenance plan
- Subcontracts the installation to a different company

## Long-Term Planning

### Maintenance Is Non-Negotiable

South Florida systems run 10-12 months per year. Twice-annual professional maintenance catches:

- Refrigerant leaks (a 10% charge loss = 20% efficiency drop)
- Electrical connection degradation (humidity corrodes contacts)
- Drain line blockages (algae growth in Florida's humidity)
- Capacitor degradation (heat accelerates failure)
- Coil cleaning (pollen, salt, and debris accumulate faster here)

### Refrigerant Transition

R-410A (Puron) is being phased down under the AIM Act. R-454B (Opteon XL41) is the primary replacement. Systems manufactured after January 2025 increasingly use R-454B.

**What this means for your purchase:**
- R-410A equipment will continue to be serviced for its lifespan
- R-410A refrigerant prices will increase as supply decreases
- R-454B systems may cost slightly more now but will be cheaper to service long-term
- If buying a system in 2026, R-454B is the forward-looking choice

### Warranty Registration

**Critical**: Most manufacturers require warranty registration within 60-90 days of installation. Without registration, your 10-year parts warranty drops to 5 years. Your contractor should handle this, but verify it yourself.

## South Florida Specific Considerations

### Salt Air Corrosion
If your home is within 5 miles of the Atlantic or Gulf coast, consider:
- Coastal-rated condenser coils (aluminum or coated copper)
- Elevated condenser pad (keeps unit above storm surge level)
- More frequent coil cleaning (quarterly vs. semi-annual)

### Hurricane Preparedness
- Condenser tie-down straps are code-required but verify installation
- Know how to safely shut down your system before a storm
- After flooding: do NOT restart until a [qualified technician](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-repair/) inspects the system

### FPL Rebates and Incentives
Florida Power & Light offers rebates for high-efficiency equipment:
- Up to $500 for qualifying high-efficiency AC systems
- Additional rebates for duct sealing
- On-bill financing programs available
- Check FPL's current offerings before purchasing
